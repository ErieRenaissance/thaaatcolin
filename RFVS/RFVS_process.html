Our Process

A proven framework for preserving manufacturing businesses and facilitating successful ownership transitions. Our temporary stewardship model supports companies through stabilization, growth, and complete transition to new owner-operators.

From Evaluation to Transition

01

Initial Evaluation & Outreach

What happens: We identify succession-challenged manufacturing companies in Northwest Pennsylvania with annual revenues of $1-5 million and strong operational fundamentals. Our team conducts preliminary research and reaches out to business owners to understand their situation and goals.

Timeline: 2-4 weeks

We look for companies with:

  • Operationally sound and profitable businesses
  • Strong product-market fit and customer relationships
  • Owner approaching retirement without clear successor
  • Meaningful employment footprint in the community
  • Potential for operational improvements and growth
02

Due Diligence & Valuation

What happens: Our team conducts comprehensive due diligence, including financial analysis, operational assessment, market positioning review, and workforce evaluation. We work with the owner to establish a fair valuation based on seller's discretionary earnings and future potential.

Timeline: 6-10 weeks

Our assessment includes:

  • Three years of financial statements and tax returns
  • Operational walkthrough and equipment assessment
  • Customer concentration and contract analysis
  • Employee skills inventory and organizational structure
  • Growth opportunities and market positioning
  • Environmental, legal, and regulatory compliance review
03

Acquisition & Transition Planning

What happens: Once terms are agreed upon, The Keystone RE:Foundry acquires the business and begins the ownership transition. The legacy owner typically remains involved in an advisory capacity with a defined consulting arrangement, ensuring knowledge transfer and continuity for customers and employees.

Timeline: 4-8 weeks for closing

Transition includes:

  • Purchase agreement and asset transfer
  • Legacy owner consulting agreement (typically 6-18 months)
  • Non-compete terms that protect business value
  • Employee communication and retention plans
  • Customer notification and relationship management
  • Initial 100-day operational stabilization roadmap
04

Temporary Stewardship & Stabilization

What happens: This is where our temporary stewardship model begins. The Keystone RE:Foundry provides hands-on operational support to stabilize the business, implement financial controls, upgrade systems, and begin identifying potential future owner-operators from within the workforce.

Timeline: 12-24 months (Phase 1 & 2)

Our stewardship includes:

  • Phase 1 - Foundation (Months 1-12): Operational assessment, financial system implementation, baseline KPI development, and initial leadership identification
  • Phase 2 - Strategic Development (Months 13-24): Process optimization, growth initiatives, management team strengthening, and formal succession candidate development
  • Regular board-level reporting and performance tracking
  • Access to specialized consultants for targeted challenges
  • Working capital management and cash flow optimization
  • Cultural alignment and employee engagement programs
05

Growth & Leadership Development

What happens: With the business stabilized, focus shifts to sustainable growth and preparing the next generation of owner-operators. Future owners receive intensive training in financial management, strategic planning, and operational leadership while taking on increasing responsibility.

Timeline: 18-36 months (Phase 3 & 4)

Development pathway includes:

  • Phase 3 - Growth Leadership (Months 25-42): Expansion initiatives, market development, full P&L responsibility, and ownership readiness assessment
  • Phase 4 - Ownership Readiness (Months 43-60): Complete operational autonomy, financing arrangements, and final transition planning
  • Formal leadership training and executive coaching
  • Financial literacy and business ownership education
  • Mentorship from RE:Foundry team and advisory partners
  • Gradual reduction of direct RE:Foundry involvement
06

Complete Exit & Ownership Transfer

What happens: The Keystone RE:Foundry facilitates complete ownership transfer to the new owner-operators. This includes structuring financing, establishing legal entities, and ensuring all stakeholders are positioned for long-term success. We exit completely, recycling capital for additional acquisitions.

Timeline: 3-6 months for final transition

Exit mechanics include:

  • Purchase agreement with new owner-operators
  • Financing support through partner lenders
  • Seller financing options to facilitate transition
  • Post-exit advisory period (typically 6-12 months)
  • Ongoing relationship for knowledge sharing
  • Capital recycling into next acquisition

What "Temporary Stewardship" Actually Means

We Are Temporary

Our goal is always to exit completely. We are not a permanent owner or operator. We serve as a bridge between the retiring founder and the next generation of leaders, then step aside completely once the transition is successful.

Hands-On Initially

In the first 12-24 months, we are actively involved in day-to-day operations, implementing improvements, and stabilizing performance. As the business strengthens and new leaders emerge, our involvement gradually decreases.

Developing From Within

We believe the best future owners are already working in the business. We identify high-potential employees, provide them with training and development, and create a pathway to ownership that would otherwise be unavailable to them.

Performance Focused

We measure success through clear KPIs including throughput, margin improvement, working capital efficiency, and cultural alignment. Our operational improvements strengthen the business for long-term viability under new ownership.

Systems & Support

We implement proven operational frameworks, financial controls, and management systems. New owners inherit not just a business, but a complete operational playbook and support network for sustained success.

Community Anchored

Throughout our stewardship, we maintain and strengthen the company's role as a community employer and economic anchor. Jobs are preserved, local suppliers are supported, and the business remains rooted in its community.

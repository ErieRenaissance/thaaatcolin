Director - Portfolio Operations

Full-Time Northwest Pennsylvania

The Refounder Venture Studio is seeking an exceptional Director of Portfolio Operations to lead operational transformation across our portfolio of acquired small manufacturing businesses in Northwest Pennsylvania. This role is critical to our mission of preserving the region's industrial foundation while demonstrating that manufacturing businesses can thrive under new ownership.

About the Role

As Director of Portfolio Operations, you will be responsible for driving operational excellence, efficiency improvements, and sustainable growth across multiple manufacturing companies. You'll work directly with company leadership, implement best practices, and ensure successful transitions while preserving the technical knowledge and workforce that makes these businesses valuable.

Key Responsibilities

Operational Leadership

  • Develop and implement operational strategies across portfolio companies to improve efficiency, quality, and profitability
  • Lead operational due diligence during acquisition processes, identifying risks and opportunities
  • Establish standardized operating procedures while respecting each company's unique culture and capabilities
  • Drive continuous improvement initiatives using lean manufacturing and Six Sigma methodologies
  • Monitor key performance indicators (KPIs) and operational metrics across the portfolio

Transition Management

  • Oversee smooth ownership transitions, ensuring business continuity and employee retention
  • Develop and execute 100-day plans for newly acquired companies
  • Facilitate knowledge transfer from retiring founders to new management teams
  • Identify and address operational gaps exposed during transition periods

Strategic Growth

  • Identify opportunities for operational improvements, capacity expansion, and market development
  • Support business development efforts by evaluating new customer opportunities and partnerships
  • Develop supply chain strategies that leverage synergies across portfolio companies
  • Lead capital investment planning for equipment upgrades and facility improvements

Team Development

  • Mentor and develop operations managers and plant leadership across portfolio companies
  • Build operational capabilities within each business to ensure long-term sustainability
  • Foster a culture of operational excellence, safety, and continuous improvement
  • Recruit and develop operational talent as the portfolio grows

Required Qualifications

  • 10+ years of progressive operations leadership experience in manufacturing environments
  • Proven track record of driving operational improvements and business transformations
  • Experience working with small to mid-sized manufacturing companies (under 100 employees)
  • Deep understanding of lean manufacturing, quality systems, and continuous improvement methodologies
  • Strong financial acumen with experience in P&L management and capital planning
  • Excellent leadership and communication skills with ability to influence at all organizational levels
  • Bachelor's degree in engineering, operations management, or related field (MBA preferred)
  • Willingness to travel regularly throughout Northwest Pennsylvania

Preferred Qualifications

  • Experience in precision machining, metal fabrication, or related manufacturing sectors
  • Private equity, venture capital, or portfolio company experience
  • Six Sigma Black Belt or equivalent certification
  • Experience with ERP implementations and manufacturing software systems
  • Background in business acquisitions or post-merger integration
  • Strong ties to the Northwest Pennsylvania region

What We Offer

  • Competitive compensation package including base salary and performance-based incentives
  • Equity participation in portfolio company success
  • Comprehensive benefits including health insurance, retirement plan, and professional development
  • Opportunity to make a meaningful impact on regional economic development
  • Autonomy to build operational systems and processes from the ground up
  • Work with diverse manufacturing businesses and learn multiple industries
  • Be part of a mission-driven organization preserving manufacturing jobs and knowledge

Our Culture

We value pragmatism, operational excellence, and respect for the people who build things. We're looking for someone who can balance the analytical rigor required for operational improvement with the emotional intelligence needed to lead through organizational change. You should be comfortable getting your hands dirty on the shop floor while also being able to present strategic recommendations to stakeholders.

Location

This role is based in Northwest Pennsylvania with regular travel to portfolio company locations throughout the region. We offer flexibility in work arrangements but expect significant time spent on-site at our manufacturing facilities.
